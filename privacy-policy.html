Trimly Privacy Policy

Last Updated: [9/12/2025]

This Privacy Policy explains how Trimly ("we", "our", "the App") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our mobile application. By using Trimly, you agree to the practices outlined in this Privacy Policy.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide Account Information: Email address and password when you sign up using Firebase Authentication. Project Data: Video editing projects, timeline metadata, draft states, saved exports, and related settings. Optional User Inputs: Feedback, error reports, in-app contact messages. We do not collect your phone contacts, photos, or media without explicit permission granted by you through your device.

2. Information Automatically Collected

2.1 Device Information We may collect non-identifying device data such as: Operating system version Device model App version Performance metrics This is used only to improve stability and performance.

2.2 Usage Analytics

If enabled, Firebase Analytics and Crashlytics track: Session counts Feature usage Crash reports Error logs These contain no personally identifying video content, only technical information.

3. How Your Data Is Used

We use your information to: Provide authentication & secure access Save drafts, projects, and video timelines Sync your project data across sessions Improve app performance Detect and fix crashes Store user-generated content Your data is never sold, never shared with advertisers, and never used to create personal profiles.

4. How Your Data Is Stored

4.1 Firebase Firestore

Stores lightweight metadata such as: project titles editing states (draft/saved) timeline structures timestamps

4.2 Firebase Storage

Stores user-uploaded project exports or optional cloud backups. Your raw video files remain on your device unless you explicitly upload them.

4.3 Local Device Storage (MMKV / Hive)

Used for: caching drafts faster load times offline editing Local caches remain on your device only.

4.4 FFmpeg Processing

Video editing and transformations occur locally on your device. No video is uploaded to any external FFmpeg server.
